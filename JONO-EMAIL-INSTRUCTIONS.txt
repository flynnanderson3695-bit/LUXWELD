Subject: LUXWELD warranty backups - your setup steps

Hi Jono,

Here's the full setup for the warranty backups. When it's done, every warranty
record (all photos and details) copies automatically to your Google Drive AND to
a hard drive - with nothing to remember day to day.

It's a mix of a few things you do, plus two quick bits Flynn does in between.
Just go in order and pause where it says WAIT.


THE PLAN AT A GLANCE
--------------------
1. YOU:  Set up the Google connection (~15 min, best on a laptop/desktop). = PART 1 below
2. YOU:  Send Flynn the two codes you get at the end of Part 1.
3. WAIT: Flynn switches the connection on (a few minutes) and tells you it's ready.
4. YOU:  Turn on the backup with one click on the website. = PART 2 below
5. YOU + FLYNN: A 2-minute step together to keep the site on 24/7 (Flynn leads this;
        it needs your card - nothing to prepare).
6. YOU:  Set up the always-on backup computer + hard drive (~10 min). = PART 3 below
7. YOU (optional): Set a stronger password. = PART 4 below

You can start PART 1 right now, on your own computer, signed in to the LUXWELD
Google account. (A laptop or desktop is much easier than a phone for this.)

If any screen looks different from these notes (Google changes their layout
sometimes), don't guess - take a screenshot and send it to Flynn. Nothing here
can break the website.


==================================================
PART 1 - Set up the Google connection  (~15 minutes)
==================================================

A. Create a project
   1. Go to  console.cloud.google.com  and sign in with the LUXWELD Google account.
   2. If it asks about terms of service, tick agree and continue.
   3. At the very top, click the project dropdown (may say "Select a project"),
      then click New Project (top right).
   4. Name: type  LUXWELD Warranty . Leave the rest as-is. Click Create.
   5. Wait a few seconds, then make sure the top dropdown shows LUXWELD Warranty
      (select it if not).

B. Turn on Google Drive
   1. In the search bar at the top, type:  Google Drive API   then press Enter.
   2. Click the result "Google Drive API".
   3. Click the blue Enable button and wait for it to finish.

C. Set up and PUBLISH the consent screen   (don't skip step 5!)
   1. In the top search bar, type:  OAuth consent screen   and open it.
      (On newer layouts it's under "Google Auth Platform" > Branding / Audience.)
   2. If asked "User Type", choose External, then Create.
   3. Fill only the required fields (marked with a red star):
        - App name: LUXWELD Warranty
        - User support email: pick your email
        - Developer contact: your email again
      Then click Save and Continue through the next pages (nothing to add there).
   4. Find the "Audience" (or "Publishing status") page.
   5. Under Publishing status it likely says "Testing". Click Publish app, then
      Confirm. It should now say "In production".
      ^ This one matters - if you skip it, the backup stops working after 7 days.

D. Create the connection
   1. In the top search bar, type:  Credentials   and open it
      (APIs & Services > Credentials).
   2. Click "+ Create Credentials", then "OAuth client ID".
   3. Application type: choose Web application.
   4. Name: LUXWELD Warranty website  (any name is fine).
   5. Scroll to "Authorised redirect URIs", click "+ Add URI", and paste this
      EXACTLY:

          https://warranty.luxweld.com.au/admin/drive/callback

   6. Click "+ Add URI" again and paste this second one too:

          https://warranty.luxweld.com.au/auth/google/callback

   7. Click Create.

E. Copy the two codes
   A window titled "OAuth client created" pops up with:
        - Client ID     (a long code ending in .apps.googleusercontent.com)
        - Client secret (a shorter code starting with GOCSPX-)
   Copy BOTH (there are copy buttons) and send them to Flynn, like you would a
   password - don't post them anywhere public. If the window closed, reopen it
   from Credentials > click your client name > the codes are on the right.

   >>> Now send Flynn the two codes, and WAIT for him to say it's ready. <<<


==================================================
PART 2 - Turn on the backup  (~2 min, AFTER Flynn says ready)
==================================================
   1. Go to  warranty.luxweld.com.au  and log in
      (email jono@luxweld.com.au; if unsure of the password, ask Flynn to reset it).
   2. Click Archive in the top menu.
   3. Find the "Google Drive backup" box near the top and click Connect Google Drive.
   4. Sign in with your Google account and click Allow / Continue.
   5. You'll come back to the Archive page and it'll say the first backup is uploading.

   Done right: open drive.google.com and within a few minutes you'll see a folder
   "LUXWELD Warranty Archive" filling up with a folder for each flashing. To find
   any record later, just type a serial number, installer name, site, or date into
   the Google Drive search bar.


==================================================
PART 3 - Always-on backup computer + hard drive  (~10 min)
==================================================
   Do this on the computer you'll leave switched on, with the 10 TB drive plugged in.
   1. Plug in the external hard drive.
   2. Go to  google.com/drive/download  and install "Google Drive for desktop".
   3. Open it and sign in with the same Google account your backups go to.
   4. Click the gear (Settings) > Preferences > Google Drive, and choose
      "Mirror files" (keeps a real full copy on the drive, not just links).
   5. When asked where to keep the files (or under Advanced settings), pick the
      external hard drive (for example  E:\LUXWELD Backup ). This same menu is
      where you'd change the drive later.
   6. Let it finish the first copy, then open the drive and check the
      "LUXWELD Warranty Archive" folder is there with photos inside.

   Keep this computer switched on (turn off "sleep" in the power settings). If it's
   ever off for a while it's fine - it catches up as soon as it's back on.


==================================================
PART 4 (optional) - Stronger password
==================================================
   Your admin login opens everything, so a longer password is safer than a short
   number. To change it: log in, click Users (top menu), and on your own account
   set a new password (a short phrase plus a number and symbol is ideal).


That's it. Once Parts 1, 2 and 3 are done, your warranty data lives in THREE
places at once - the live website, your Google Drive, and your physical hard
drive - all kept up to date automatically.
